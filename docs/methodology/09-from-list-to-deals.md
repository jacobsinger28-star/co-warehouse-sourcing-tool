# From List to Deals

The list is the input to outreach, not a replacement for it. The engine produces
and ranks the list. The calls, the letters, and the relationships stay yours.
What changes is where your hours go. Instead of burning half the week on
research, you spend it on verified connects, better-timed letters to your
top-scored owners, and the conversations that actually close.

---

## A cadence that works

- **Priority owners (50+):** a personal call and a property-specific letter this
  week. These are the owners the signals say are most likely to move.
- **Watch owners (25-49):** a light mail sequence, re-scored monthly. A new lien
  or a missed tax payment can move a Watch owner to Priority overnight, and the
  re-score is what catches it.
- **Background (under 25):** no proactive outreach. They stay in the universe so
  that when a signal fires, they surface on their own.

Personalize only on facts the pipeline established (hold period, asset specifics,
submarket activity), never invented familiarity, and never a dollar figure in the
first touch.

---

## Re-run, do not rebuild

The value compounds when you run the engine on a schedule, not once. Rerun FIND
and SCORE monthly on your submarkets. The universe is finite, so you are not
re-discovering it, you are watching it change: new delinquencies, new liens,
maturities coming due, owners crossing a tenure threshold. The first credible call
to a newly motivated owner usually wins.

---

## The maintenance truth

Be honest with yourself about upkeep, whether you run this by hand or wire it into
an agent.

- **County sites change URLs and formats without notice.** A pull that worked last
  month can break. You re-point it.
- **Signals drift.** What predicted sellers last year may weaken. You reweight from
  your wins.
- **Owner data goes stale at about 22% a year.** You refresh on a schedule.

Upkeep is not a bug, it is the cost of a pipeline that keeps working: the feeds
stay running, the scoring stays tuned, and the owner data stays fresh. Budget for
it. This is the build, not a magic button, and the work of keeping it sharp is
exactly what makes it an edge instead of a one-time list.
