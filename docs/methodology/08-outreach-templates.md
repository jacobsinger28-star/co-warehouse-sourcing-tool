# Outreach Templates

Three pieces per target, drafted in Stage 4, edited by you. Merge slots are in
{braces}. The rule behind all three: every personalized line traces to a record
the pipeline established. If you cannot point to the record, cut the line. No
invented familiarity, no broker cliches, and no dollar figure in the first touch.
The number is the reason they call you back.

These read for any asset class. The merge slots carry the asset specifics, so the
same three templates work whether you are writing to an apartment owner or a
small-bay industrial owner.

---

## Owner letter (print, signed, mailed)

```
{owner_first_name} {owner_last_name}
{owner_mailing_address}

Dear {owner_first_name},

I am writing about {property_address}, the {asset_descriptor} you have owned
since {acquisition_year}. I will keep this short.

My firm acquires {asset_type} in {submarket_name}, and your property fits what we
close on. This is not a blast letter: we looked at {specific_property_fact, e.g.
"the 48 units and the 2019 roof work in the permit record"} before writing to you.

If you have ever wondered what the property would trade for in today's market, I
would welcome a 15-minute conversation. No listing, no obligation, and I will
share our read on value either way.

{sender_signoff}
{sender_name}
{sender_firm} | {sender_phone} | {sender_email}
```

## Email (first touch)

```
Subject: {property_address} - owner question

{owner_first_name},

Quick note about {property_address}. We acquire {asset_type} in {submarket_name}
and your property fits our mandate.

You have held it since {acquisition_year}, so you know what it is better than
anyone. If a conversation about today's value is ever useful, I am easy to reach,
and happy to share our underwriting view either way.

{sender_name}
{sender_firm} | {sender_phone}

If you would rather not hear from me again, reply "no thanks" and that is that.
```

## Call script

```
OPEN
"Hi {owner_first_name}, this is {sender_name} with {sender_firm}. Do you have a
quick minute? I am calling about {property_address}."

WHY THEM (one breath, facts only)
"We acquire {asset_type} in {submarket_name}. Your property came up because
{specific_fact, e.g. 'you have held it since 2011 and it is exactly the vintage
we buy'}. This is not a listing pitch."

THE ASK
"I am not going to throw a number at you on a cold call. If you are open to it, I
would like 15 minutes to share how we would look at the property and hear whether
a sale is even interesting this year."

OBJECTION - "Not for sale."
"Completely fair. Most owners we end up working with said the same at first. Would
it be useful to have our view on value anyway, no strings?"

OBJECTION - "Send me something."
"Will do. I will email a short note on our mandate today, no attachment spam.
What is the best email - I have {email_on_file}?"

CLOSE
"Appreciate the time, {owner_first_name}. You will have my details either way. If
timing changes in a year, the offer of a conversation stands."
```

---

**Compliance reminders (non-negotiable).** Scrub against the National Do Not Call
Registry and your state registries before dialing. Emails carry truthful sender
identity and the opt-out line. The first touch never includes a dollar figure.
None of this is legal advice; when in doubt, ask your counsel.
