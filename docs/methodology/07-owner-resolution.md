# Owner Resolution, and an Honest Ceiling

Scoring tells you who to call. Resolution tells you how to reach them. This is
where the system earns its trust, and where honesty matters most.

---

## The method: trace the entity to a human, with evidence

Most off-market owners hold through an LLC or a trust. The job is to get from the
entity on the deed to the person who can say yes, without guessing.

The system works the chain through public records:

- **Secretary of State filings** name managers, members, and registered agents.
- **Deed history** shows who signed at acquisition.
- **Mailing addresses** tie entities to people.

The non-negotiable rule is the evidence chain. The system asserts a human only
with a documented chain, each link citing the record it came from:

```
Maple Holdings LLC
  -> Secretary of State filing names manager Jane R. Maple
  -> same mailing address as the deed signatory on the 2019 transfer
  -> Jane R. Maple (HIGH confidence)
```

No chain, no name. Two rules ride on top of it:

- **A registered agent is not an owner.** Distinguishing the agent from the
  principal is the most common skip-trace error. The system is built to separate
  them.
- **Confidence is graded and carried forward.** Every resolution is HIGH, MEDIUM,
  or LOW. Unresolvable entities are marked UNRESOLVED with a note on which record
  would resolve them, not quietly dropped.

---

## The honest ceiling

Tracing an LLC or trust to a verified, reachable human is not a solved problem.
Be clear-eyed about the numbers:

- From registry data alone, entity-to-person resolution runs about **35 to 50%**.
- With fallbacks and skip-trace it climbs, but right-party contact for this kind
  of cohort realistically tops out around **50 to 60%**.
- Anyone promising you 95% is selling you something.

Two more truths that change how you treat the list:

- **Contact data decays about 22% a year.** A list is a snapshot, not an asset
  you buy once. Refresh on a schedule.
- **Bad contact data quietly taxes every outreach program**, with a meaningful
  share of dialing time spent on numbers that no longer work. That is exactly the
  tax a verified, evidence-chained list removes.

---

## Where contact data comes from

The system does not invent phone numbers or emails. It resolves the human from
public records and hands you the inputs your enrichment provider needs (full name,
mailing address, linked entity). You run enrichment with your own provider, and
the system carries the provider's verification flags forward verbatim, never
upgrading "unverified" to "verified."

The identity-match rule guards the dangerous failure, which is not a missing
number but the right number for the wrong John Smith. A returned contact is only
trusted when a corroborating field beyond the name (mailing address, linked
entity, geography) matches.

---

## What to verify before outreach

Run this on your priority batch, every time:

- Every evidence chain reads cleanly, each link citing a record.
- The named human is a principal, not a registered agent.
- Common-name collisions resolved (the right John Smith).
- No UNRESOLVED or LOW-confidence entity is in the batch going out.
- Contact verification flags are provider-verified, not assumed.
- The call list is scrubbed against the National DNC Registry and your state
  registry before you dial.

You own the pipeline that produces this list, and the judgment that sends it. The
ceiling is real; planning around it, verifying before you dial, and refreshing on
a schedule is what separates a working system from a list you bought once and
trusted too long.
