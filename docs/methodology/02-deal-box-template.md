# The Deal Box Template

Fill every field. The system reads this at every stage, and it works for any
asset class. Blanks become noise, and a sloppy deal box produces a long list of
properties you do not want. Save it and add it to your Claude Project as a file.

---

## Mandate

- **Asset class:** (e.g. multifamily 20-80 units / neighborhood retail /
  small-bay industrial / manufactured-housing parks / infill land)
- **Size band:** (units, square feet, pad count, or acreage, whichever fits the
  asset class)
- **Vintage or condition band:** (e.g. 1975-2005, or "value-add, deferred
  maintenance ok")
- **Deal size:** (e.g. $2M-$12M total capitalization)
- **Target geography:** (county, submarket boundary, or map link; be specific.
  One submarket at a time beats one whole metro on day one)
- **Return target:** (e.g. 7%+ stabilized yield, or value-add to a 12% IRR)

## Owner profile

- **Keep:** LLCs, trusts, family partnerships, long-hold individuals (10y+)
- **Drop:** REITs, institutions, pension funds, national operators, merchant
  builders
- **Signals worth ranking up:** (pick the ones that fit your asset class from the
  signal library in file 04, e.g. out-of-state owner, long hold with low basis,
  loan maturity approaching, tax delinquency, deferred maintenance, aging owner
  with no clear succession)

## Hard no's

- (e.g. flood zone, ground lease, pre-1960 unreinforced masonry, known
  environmental flags, HOA-controlled, anything you will not underwrite)

## Sponsor fit

- One paragraph: who you are, what you actually close on, and the proof you
  close. The system uses this in outreach drafts so owners hear a credible buyer,
  not a list blast. Be specific: "We have closed 11 deals in this submarket since
  2019 and we close all-cash in 30 days" beats "we are an active buyer."

---

**Why this matters.** The accuracy of everything downstream comes from this page
being specific. The same engine that sources apartments sources industrial and
land; what changes is the deal box and the signals you turn on. Tune it against
your own closed deals over time. The deals you won tell you which signals were
real for your strategy.
